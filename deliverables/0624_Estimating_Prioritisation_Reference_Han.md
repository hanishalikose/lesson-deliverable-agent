__Topic__
Estimating and Prioritisation in Project Management.

__Key Concepts__
The core ideas of estimating and prioritisation in project management, including five estimating methods: Planning Poker, Three-Point Estimation, Analogous Estimation, Team Estimation Game, and Expert Judgement. These methods differ in whether the estimate is made by the team together or by an individual, and whether it produces a relative size or an absolute figure.

__PM Takeaways__
Product managers should use these estimating methods to facilitate collaborative estimation and prioritisation of work items, and to provide credible estimates to stakeholders. They should also be aware of the advantages and limitations of each method, and choose the most suitable one based on the project's needs and stage.

__Learnings__
> A key takeaway is that an estimate is not a commitment, and should be communicated as a range with a confidence level, and refined as more information becomes available.
